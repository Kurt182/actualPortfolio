About Me

My Coding Story
I have several friends that are coders. All of them became coders via the University/Computer Science degree route. I never really understood what they did or how they worked with code. Once I heard one of them say that all a person really needed to get a job as a coder was a good portfolio. This made me curious. Was this something I could do? I started asking questions. How could I learn how to code? Going back to College for a Computer Science degree was not an option. The two road blocks to my quest were time and money. Since all my friends came through the University system, none of them had a realistic idea of how I could learn to code. I started doing a little online research and came accross a coding event. It was the 2014 Hour of Code. I saw a video by then President Obama talking about the event. I then found out my local event location was at Eleven-Fifty Academy. I had known of the founder of Eleven-Fifty from when I was a waiter. In fine dining restaurants, most of the local successful business people are well known and Scott Jones was known as "the guy that invented voicemail". In 2014 Scott Jones founded Eleven-Fifty Academy and because I knew of his success, I thought that this could be a great learning experience. I remember that day in December 2014 well. Because Eleven-Fifty was just getting started, the event was held in Mr Jones' Carmel mansion. It's hard to forget an event like that held in such extravengent environment.
